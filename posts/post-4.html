9 Data visualization tools: pros and cons

Published by Daniel Fernandes on March 09th, 2024

Introduction

I will present a comparison between some of the best data visualization tools available with some pros and cons. It is important to note that choosing the best data visualization tool depends on the specific needs of the project, budget, team expertise and integration with other tools and systems. It is therefore advisable to test and evaluate each of them, along with their pros and cons and evaluate how they meet the project requirements before making a final decision.

Below are the strengths of each tool:

 

  1. Tableau: Intuitive interface, wide variety of visualization options, excellent data integration capability, strong community support, and versions for desktop and online.
  2. Power BI (Microsoft): Seamless integration with Microsoft products, advanced AI features, competitive pricing, effective collaboration, and interactive visualizations.
  3. QlikView / Qlik Sense: Flexible data association, excellent performance, capability to create interactive applications, and great data exploration.
  4. Google Data Studio: Free, integration with Google products, easy collaboration, and simple online sharing.
  5. D3.js: Extremely flexible and customizable, ideal for developers, and support for custom visualizations.
  6. Plotly: Data visualization library in Python, R, and JavaScript, advanced interactivity, open-source, and support for 3D graphics.
  7. Looker: Solid integration with databases, built-in analysis features, and emphasis on collaboration.
  8. Highcharts: JavaScript library for interactive charts, wide range of chart types, detailed documentation.
  9. Sisense: Good data management capability, scalability, integration with various data sources, AI support.

Below are the drawbacks of each tool:

  1. Tableau: Relatively high price for businesses, learning curve for advanced features.
  2. Power BI (Microsoft): Limitations in the free version and limited customization features.
  3. QlikView / Qlik Sense: High price and steep learning curve for beginners.
  4. Google Data Studio: Fewer advanced features compared to some paid tools.
  5. D3.js: Steep learning curve, not as user-friendly for non-developers.
  6. Plotly: Requires programming skills for advanced usage.
  7. Looker: Higher price, less flexibility compared to some tools.
  8. Highcharts: Not a complete solution and depends on coding for implementation.
  9. Sisense: Higher price for businesses, learning curve.

So, can you already get an idea of which tool is ideal for your project?
